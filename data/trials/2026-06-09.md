## Chinese Source Trial

The Chinese media watch pool was expanded in `data/sources/china-leads.yaml`.

`npx pnpm ingest:china-leads` generated 92 search tasks.

Current Chinese strategy:

- Treat Chinese media as a discovery lane, not RSS-only ingestion.
- Use search queries, public listing pages, and reader submissions to discover leads.
- AI should extract claim, people/place/date/action/result, evidence gaps, and risk.
- Human review decides whether a lead becomes a published item.

Early risk pattern:

- Many Chinese results are policy announcements, enterprise ESG copy, official-style reports, or old articles.
- The useful items are likely hidden among public-interest reporting, local follow-ups, NGO/project updates, and concrete service improvements.

## Workflow Finding

`scripts/ingest/score-candidates.ts` currently writes `data/candidates/YYYY-MM-DD.scored.json`, but the full run did not create separate `data/candidates/YYYY-MM-DD.json` and `data/rejected/YYYY-MM-DD.json` files.

Next improvement:

- Add a deterministic export/reroute command that reads `.scored.json` and writes candidate/rejected pools for daily review.

Implemented next:

- `npx pnpm ingest:export-scored -- --date 2026-06-09` exports review pools without calling the AI provider.

## Day 1 Conclusion

The project can find enough English candidates for a daily review queue, but not all candidates are publishable. A realistic daily output should remain flexible:

- publish fewer when only a few items are strong;
- do not publish weak stories to hit a quota;
- treat Upworthy and Chinese media as lead-only unless verification is strong.
