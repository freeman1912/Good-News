# Chinese Source Shortlist · 2026-06-09

This is a manual Chinese-source trial shortlist. It is not a published news file.

## Updated Screening Rule

Chinese leads are judged by visible result and future trackability, not by whether
the headline sounds positive.

Routes:

- `publishable`: result is already visible and the source trail can be checked.
- `follow-up`: promising, but today it is only approved, launched, announced, or single-source.
- `rejected`: inaccessible, propaganda-like, purely ceremonial, or not practically trackable.

## Rejected Or Deprioritized Patterns

- Source cannot be opened or traced.
- The item is only approval, launch, signing, ceremony, or meeting.
- It has no visible beneficiary or result.
- It is official-only or corporate-only and has no follow-up path.
- It is a publicity article whose main value is brand, institution, or official image.

## Trial Conclusion

Under the stricter model, the first Chinese test does not produce a full daily
issue. It produces:

- 1 likely publishable lead if source trail can be reopened and cross-checked;
- 1 medical/public-aid lead that may become publishable with evidence;
- 6 follow-up leads that should be tracked, not published today.

This is the right failure mode. The project should publish fewer items rather
than convert announcements and official/corporate releases into fake certainty.
